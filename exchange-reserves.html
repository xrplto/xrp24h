What Are XRP Exchange Reserves?

XRP exchange reserves represent the total amount of XRP held in wallets controlled by cryptocurrency exchanges. This metric is important for understanding:

  • Selling Pressure: High reserves may indicate more XRP available to sell
  • Accumulation: Declining reserves suggest investors are moving XRP to self-custody
  • Market Liquidity: Exchange reserves affect how easily large trades can be executed

Why Track Exchange Reserves?

When XRP leaves exchanges, it typically means holders are moving to long-term storage, reducing potential sell pressure. Conversely, increasing reserves might signal upcoming selling activity.

Current trend: Exchange reserves have been declining, suggesting accumulation by long-term holders.

How much XRP is currently on exchanges?
As of today, approximately 4.5 billion XRP is held across major exchanges, representing about 4.5% of the total supply.
Which exchange holds the most XRP?
Binance typically holds the largest XRP reserves, followed by Kraken and Bitstamp. These reserves fluctuate based on trading activity and user deposits/withdrawals.
Is declining exchange reserve bullish for XRP?
Generally, yes. Declining reserves indicate that holders are moving XRP to personal wallets, suggesting confidence in long-term holding rather than selling. However, this should be considered alongside other market factors.
How much XRP does Ripple own?
Ripple holds approximately 40-45 billion XRP in escrow, with a portion released monthly. This is separate from exchange reserves and represents the company's holdings.
